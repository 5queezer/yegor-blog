## Summary
In this insightful interview with Yegor Bugayenko, Andrey Sebrant, Director of Strategic Marketing at Yandex, explores the intersection of artificial intelligence, corporate culture, and the future of labor. A central theme is the distinction between intelligence and personality; Sebrant argues that while AI is becoming incredibly intelligent, it lacks human-like motivation and "selfhood." This leads into a discussion on AGI, where he suggests the next leap isn't just better language models, but "world models" that understand physical reality.

The conversation shifts to the practicalities of robotics, specifically the "Drone vs. Courier" debate. Sebrant notes that in dense urban environments like Moscow, wheeled "rovers" are often more viable than flying drones due to regulatory and navigational complexities. Regarding management, Sebrant provides a deep dive into Yandex's internal "Phinney" system and peer-review calibrations. He emphasizes that a modern IT leader's role is to cultivate an environment where talent can thrive, rather than acting as a traditional supervisor. 

Finally, they touch upon the socioeconomic impact of technology. Sebrant offers a grounded perspective on the "startup mania," stating that a functioning society requires people to maintain critical infrastructure just as much as it needs entrepreneurs. For software engineers, the message is clear: the role is evolving from writing manual code to managing complex AI-driven systems. Quality remains paramount, especially in high-stakes fields like aerospace, where a single bug can negate decades of effort.

## Key Insights
- "Intelligence is not the same as personality; AI can be brilliant without having its own will or motivation."
- "The programmer of the future is an orchestrator of AI agents and complex systems, not just a manual coder."
- "A leader in a modern tech company isn't an overseer, but an architect of the environment where experts work."
- "Not everyone should be a startup founder; society needs people who 'drive the subway trains' to keep the world running."

## Relevance: 4/5
High relevance for software engineers and managers. The discussion on Yandex’s peer-review systems, the evolving role of the developer in the age of LLMs, and the focus on code quality in critical systems offers direct architectural and leadership value.
