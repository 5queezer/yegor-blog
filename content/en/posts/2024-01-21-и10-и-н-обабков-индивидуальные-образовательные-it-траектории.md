## Summary
In this interview with Yegor Bugayenko, Ilya Obabkov, the director of IRIT-RTF at the Ural Federal University (UrFU), discusses the radical transformation of higher education to meet the demands of the modern IT industry. The central theme is the implementation of Individual Educational Trajectories (IET), a model that moves away from rigid, one-size-fits-all curricula. Instead, students are empowered to choose specific modules and difficulty levels, effectively tailoring their own professional development in areas like DevOps, Artificial Intelligence, or Frontend development.

Obabkov emphasizes that this flexibility is essential because the IT market evolves much faster than government educational standards. To manage this complexity, the university utilizes a digital platform to track "digital footprints," providing data-driven guidance for students. The conversation also highlights "Project-Based Learning," where students work on real-world tasks provided by industry partners like Kontur, Yandex, and Sber. This approach bridges the gap between academic theory and industry practice. Despite the push for practicality, Obabkov argues that a university must remain a center for fundamental science and R&D to maintain its identity. Bugayenko challenges these ideas by questioning how to measure the quality of code and the ultimate success of graduates in such a flexible system. Ultimately, the interview presents a vision of "University 2.0"—an ecosystem designed for growth rather than a mere factory for "coders."

## Key Insights
- "The IT market changes faster than state educational standards can be updated."
- "A university is not just a 'programming bootcamp'; it is an environment for growth and research."
- "Individual trajectories require the student to take responsibility for their own professional choices."
- "Scaling flexible education is impossible without a digital platform that tracks the student's 'digital footprint'."

## Relevance: 4/5
This discussion is highly relevant to team management and software engineering education. It explores how to foster specialized talent and manage the transition from academic learning to professional production environments.
