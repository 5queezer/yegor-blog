## Summary
In this interview, Yegor Bugayenko discusses the current landscape of AI and academia with Ivan Oseledets, a leading mathematician and head of AIRI. Oseledets provides a grounded perspective on Large Language Models (LLMs), describing them as "C-grade students" that prioritize safe, generalized answers over deep reasoning. He warns of "model collapse," where training AI on synthetic data leads to a degradation of quality. 

The conversation shifts to scientific productivity, where Oseledets critiques the over-reliance on the H-index, citing Goodhart’s Law: when a metric becomes a goal, it ceases to be a good metric. He emphasizes that the ability to effectively "sell" and write a scientific paper is often the primary bottleneck for researchers today. Additionally, they discuss the geopolitical challenges for Russian scientists, specifically regarding international visas and the difficulties of maintaining global collaborations. Finally, Oseledets reflects on his work with Tensor Train decomposition and its unexpected foundational role in modern machine learning.

## Key Insights
- "LLMs are like C-grade students: they give very safe, average answers to avoid being wrong, rather than demonstrating true understanding."
- "Goodhart's Law in science: Once the H-index became the primary goal for researchers, it lost its value as a genuine measure of scientific impact."
- "Scientific writing is the ultimate bottleneck; a researcher's impact is capped by their ability to communicate and 'sell' their findings to the community."
- "The risk of 'model collapse' is real: training future AI models on the output of current ones creates a feedback loop that erodes data diversity."

High relevance for software engineers and managers regarding the future of AI tools, the importance of data quality, and the management of high-performing research teams.
