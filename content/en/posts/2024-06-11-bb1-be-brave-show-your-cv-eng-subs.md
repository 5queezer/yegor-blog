## Summary
In this inaugural session of the "Be Brave" series, Yegor Bugayenko challenges software engineers to stop treating their resumes as private documents and instead subject them to public scrutiny. He argues that a CV is fundamentally a marketing brochure, not a detailed biography. Its primary purpose is to sell your professional services in a matter of seconds. 

Yegor emphasizes several "non-negotiable" rules: the resume must be strictly one page long, it must be 100% honest, and it must provide verifiable proof of expertise through public platforms like GitHub and Stack Overflow. He critiques common mistakes, such as listing too many irrelevant technologies or failing to demonstrate business value. The ultimate goal is to transition from being a "commodity coder" to a "market-recognized superstar" who can command higher rates due to visible, proven competence.

## Key Insights
- "Your CV is a marketing tool designed to sell you, not a history book of your life."
- "If you cannot fit your entire professional career onto a single page, it indicates a failure in scope management and prioritization."
- "Public profiles on GitHub and Stack Overflow are worth more than a thousand words of self-praise in a document."
- "Absolute honesty is mandatory; you must be prepared to defend every single word on your resume under technical pressure."

## Relevance: 5/5
Extremely relevant for career development and personal branding in software engineering. It connects technical excellence with market visibility and professional accountability.
