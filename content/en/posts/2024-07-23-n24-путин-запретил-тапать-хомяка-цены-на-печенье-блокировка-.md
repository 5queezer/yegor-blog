## Summary
In episode N24 of his news series, Yegor Bugayenko dives into a variety of pressing IT news, industry shifts, and modern corporate culture trends. He begins by addressing the widespread hype surrounding "Hamster Kombat", famously summarized as "tapping the hamster." Yegor strongly urges software developers to stop wasting their precious time on mindless cryptocurrency games and gamified distractions. Instead, he advocates for a return to actual software engineering, suggesting that developers should invest their free time in contributing to open-source projects or improving their technical skills. 

Moving on to corporate benefits, Yegor discusses the metaphorical and literal "prices of cookies," observing that the golden era of lavish corporate perks, unlimited snacks, and relaxed environments in the tech industry is rapidly coming to an end. Tech companies are shifting their focus toward strict productivity and measurable results rather than coddling their employees. 

Addressing the geopolitical climate, including YouTube throttling and ongoing IT sanctions in Russia, Yegor advises engineers to remain deeply pragmatic. He notes that while sanctions make daily work more difficult, a true engineer’s job is to deliver value regardless of external obstacles. He suggests using VPNs, or better yet, using the lack of video content as a perfect excuse to read classic technical books. Finally, he delivers a harsh critique of modern corporate inclusivity and DEI (Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion) initiatives. Yegor maintains his longstanding view that software development must remain a strict meritocracy, where engineers are hired and evaluated solely based on the quality of their object-oriented code, rather than arbitrary diversity quotas.

## Key Insights
- "Stop tapping hamsters and start writing real code; your time is better spent contributing to open source."
- "The era of free corporate cookies is over. The market now demands results, not pampered children."
- "If YouTube is blocked, read a book. An engineer's job is to deliver value regardless of the political environment."
- "Code has no gender or race. We need strict meritocracy, not inclusivity quotas."
