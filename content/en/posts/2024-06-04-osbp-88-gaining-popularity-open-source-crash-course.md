## Summary
In the final installment of the OSBP series, Yegor Bugayenko dives into the mechanics of gaining popularity for an Open Source project. He argues that visibility is a deliberate outcome of specific activities rather than a happy accident. A core premise is that speed of response to issues and pull requests is the single most important factor for contributor retention; a stagnant repository quickly loses its community. Yegor references academic studies to explain that GitHub Stars function as social proof and currency within the ecosystem, signaling that a project is worth a developer's time.

To systematically build popularity, Yegor offers four key pillars. First, "Adding Sparkle" through repository badges (build status, coverage, version) significantly boosts perceived professionalism. Second, maintaining momentum is vital—frequent, smaller commits signal a healthy, active project more effectively than sporadic large updates. Third, "Vitality" should be cultivated through regular version releases and public announcements. Finally, he emphasizes the necessity of active promotion. Developers should leverage platforms like X (Twitter), Reddit, and Hacker News to "make some noise" and share their progress. Ultimately, Yegor frames Open Source as a social endeavor where the vitality of the repository and the responsiveness of its maintainer are just as critical as the technical implementation.

## Key Insights
- "Speed of response is the most critical factor for contributor retention."
- "GitHub stars are the currency of social proof in the developer community."
- "A project with 100 small commits looks much healthier than one with a single giant update."
- "Open Source is a social endeavor; you must be willing to make some noise."
