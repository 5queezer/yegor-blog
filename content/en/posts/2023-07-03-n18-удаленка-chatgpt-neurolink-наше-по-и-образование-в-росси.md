## Summary
In this episode of his news series, Yegor Bugayenko explores the shifting landscape of the IT industry, focusing on the obsolescence of traditional offices and the disruptive potential of AI. He argues that physical offices have become unnecessary artifacts of the past, as modern coordination tools allow for full efficiency in distributed teams using nothing more than a stable Wi-Fi connection and video conferencing. 

A major portion of the discussion is dedicated to ChatGPT and Large Language Models (LLMs). Yegor posits a provocative shift in the development lifecycle: instead of one high-quality, expensive Pull Request (PR) from a human developer, the future might involve 15 "messy" AI-generated PRs. He suggests that the cost of automatically checking and merging these rapid iterations will be lower than a single day of a programmer's salary, fundamentally changing how we perceive code "elegance" versus "throughput."

Regarding education, Bugayenko is critical of the current Russian academic system, highlighting the low prestige and salaries of professors. He argues that the market is currently over-saturated with over-educated specialists (Bachelors and Masters) who are overqualified for common tasks like mobile game development. He also touches upon Neurolink, describing it as a terrifying yet fascinating step toward a future reminiscent of Victor Pelevin’s novels, starting with medical applications but leading to unpredictable social shifts. Finally, he provides updates on his object-oriented language, EOLANG, which continues to implement the principles of his "Elegant Objects" philosophy.

## Key Insights
- **The End of Offices:** Physical workspaces are outdated; coordination is now purely digital and decentralized.
- **AI Efficiency vs. Human Quality:** It is economically more viable to process numerous low-quality AI contributions than to pay for one "perfect" human contribution.
- **Educational Mismatch:** There is a significant gap between academic over-education and the practical, often simpler, needs of the software industry.
- **The Pelevin Future:** Neurolink represents a shift from medical utility to a potentially disturbing new social reality of human-machine integration.

## Relevance: 4/5
High relevance for team leads and developers interested in the future of AI-assisted coding, the transition to permanent remote work models, and the philosophical critique of modern OOP through EOLANG.
