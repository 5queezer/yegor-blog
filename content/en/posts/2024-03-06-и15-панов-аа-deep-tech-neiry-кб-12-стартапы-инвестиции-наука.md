## Summary
In this extensive interview with Yegor Bugayenko, Alexander Panov, the founder of Neiry and the KB-12 marketing group, shares his insights into building Deep Tech businesses. The primary focus is on Neiry, an ambitious project developing non-invasive brain-computer interfaces (EEG-equipped headphones). Panov explains that in a high-tech product, the core value lies not in the hardware itself, but in the sophisticated signal-processing algorithms that monitor a user's psycho-emotional state in real-time. This technology opens up vast possibilities, from optimizing individual productivity to implementing industrial safety systems that prevent human error caused by fatigue or lack of focus.

Panov also discusses his unique business philosophy of separating projects into "dividend-driven" and "venture-driven" categories. He argues that having a stable, profitable business (like KB-12) allows an entrepreneur to take risks in long-term R&D projects that require significant investment with no guarantee of quick returns. Addressing the role of the state, Panov notes that in the Deep Tech sector, government support and grants are often critical, as the private venture capital market is often unequipped for such long-term investment cycles.

Special attention is paid to team management and hiring. Panov believes that internal motivation and passion are far more important than formal education or degrees. He offers valuable advice to students: avoid trying to "hack" the system by skipping vital life stages. In his view, only a consistent progression through all stages of personal and professional growth creates a well-rounded individual capable of building great products. Overall, the interview serves as a comprehensive guide on how to balance a scientific approach and engineering culture with pragmatic business leadership.

## Key Insights
* "The value is not in the hardware, but in the indices we extract from the noise."
* "Separate your business into dividend-making engines and venture-driven rockets."
* "Don't try to skip life stages; experience is an evolutionary process."
* "Russia is excellent at engineering innovation but struggles with mass production; keep the IP local and outsource the manufacturing."
