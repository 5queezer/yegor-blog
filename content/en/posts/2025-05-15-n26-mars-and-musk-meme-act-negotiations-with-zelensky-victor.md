## Summary
In this N26 episode, Yegor Bugayenko dissects the current state of global politics, technology, and justice. He begins with a harsh critique of the U.S. "MEME Act," viewing it as a symptom of a degrading capitalist system where rules are bent for personal gain. He then moves to the aerospace industry, discussing the NASA Transition Authorization Act. Yegor argues that capping contracts at 50% to prevent a SpaceX monopoly is a counterproductive move that prioritizes political optics over technical and economic efficiency, effectively forcing NASA to use less capable providers.

The core of the discussion revolves around the Russia-Ukraine conflict. Yegor is deeply cynical about the shift toward peace negotiations with Zelensky, which he labels a "dogovorniak" (backroom deal). He points out the logical disconnect between the high human cost of the "special military operation" and the sudden move toward a political division of influence without transparent explanations to the public who suffered the losses.

Technologically, Yegor explores the rise of "Robo Sapiens." He highlights a courtroom case where AI recreated a deceased person's testimony, sparking a philosophical debate on whether digital consciousness can ever truly replace biological life. Finally, he addresses the prosecution of the Taganskoye organized crime group, arguing that selective justice is hypocritical if it doesn't also target the Yeltsin-era officials who enabled such criminal structures.

## Key Insights
- "The U.S. system is degrading when laws are made solely to stop individual politicians from shilling meme coins."
- "SpaceX is the definition of efficiency; capping their NASA contracts at 50% is a deliberate choice to embrace inefficiency for political comfort."
- "A 'backroom deal' in war is a sign of disrespect to those who fought for the original, non-negotiable stated goals."
- "Robo Sapiens represent a future where the boundary between biological and digital data becomes legally and philosophically irrelevant."
